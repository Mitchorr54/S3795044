My Project idea

Overview:
The idea in which I have come up with and that I am passionate about is to develop a smartphone app, I want this app to be targeted towards Indigenous communities. The idea is to promote connectivity and inclusiveness between communities and to also promote the solidification of Indigenous language in a written format to preserve and promote the individual languages for future generations.

Motivation:
My motivation behind the app is to create a social enterprise, one that has a particular focus on the recruitment and the use of Indigenous people to develop, manage and promote the app. Whilst the app will be useful, my main focus would be to work on promoting and increasing computer literacy and creating jobs on remote Indigenous communities. I have chosen an app as this will allow a lower cost entry point to use the app over a computer and it's known 'just 6 per cent of residents in some remote Indigenous communities even have a computer' (Tugwell 2011, No guarantee, para. 2).

Description:
Currently, in the majority of the Indigenous communities in which I work the use of older technology is common. An example is the use of fax machines to distribute important community notifications to other communities, such as funeral notifications or community closure notices. If these notices are missed by the receiving communities this could result for example in a person not participating in Sorry Business which is an extremely important cultural event for some Aboriginal communities.

My idea involves the updating of this protocol by allowing community notifications to be provided in a digital format instantaneously and to notify the end user with a push notification. A further benefit to this idea is that it doesn't only allow for communities and community members to access this vital information it will also allow for community stakeholders who may not be present on the community to receive these notifications and allow for them to remain up to date with what is happening. For example, with my job, we are required to travel to remote communities via vehicle this could require us driving 2+ days from Alice Springs to reach a community, usually prior to departure we need to contact the community to ensure that it is safe for us to visit the community, ensuring there is no cultural business happening or that the community isn't closed to outsiders. With the app, we could feel somewhat confident that we have access to up to date information on our travels ensuring we don’t encroach on sensitive community business.

Further to my idea above, I want to develop the app so that the end user can select the language in which they want the app to be displayed with the main focus being that the app can be displayed in an Indigenous language. According to the Australian Bureau of Statistics (2016), there are about 150 Native languages spoken.
Whilst the community notification aspect of the project is the current main idea, with the project being a social enterprise, I would propose that the app would develop over the course of its life to incorporate different elements and would hope to see the further development ideas come from the consultation with end users and communities to advocate for the changes they want to the app.

Issues that could arise:
- Indigenous languages are complex and some of the languages have no written form, this means that whilst some Indigenous people can speak their language they may be unable to read it.
- Cultural sensitivities need to be taken into account, for example with Indigenous funerals and in some Aboriginal communities mentioning a deceased person's first name is culturally insensitive.
- Indigenous recruitment can be complex and a lot of upskilling to achieve the indigenous recruitment goal is going to take time to achieve.

Tools required:
I would aim to develop the application for use initially on Android devices this means I would aim to develop the program using the Java programming language.
Software required for development:
- Integrated Development Environment (IDE) for Java: Eclipse IDE
- Windows operating system
Hardware required for development:
- Laptop or desktop PC
- Android device for testing developed product

Skills required:
Some of the skills that I have identified for the development of the product are:
- In-depth knowledge of Java programming to develop software for product
- Knowledge of how an Indigenous community works
- Indigenous language subject matter experts
- Indigenous cultural awareness
- Knowledge of product testing
- Marketing skills
I currently only possess 2 of the skills identified and would need to draw on information that I could source online and around me, I do also note that with the language side of the app there are numerous indigenous language apps on the market in which information could potentially be drawn upon.

Outcome
The outcome of the development of this app could be considered profound, I think that by looking forward to the future and realising that IT is the way forward. This can lead to empowerment and development of the most disadvantaged Australians, the other aspect I like about this idea is that it can offer the community members that develop the necessary IT skills to be able to work from their home community and remain attached to their country and keep their sense of identity whilst also being a productive member of their identity as a whole.
